# Design Philosophy

## Purpose

OpsForge is built to teach and document systems engineering through structured reasoning rather than memorization.

The project is designed around a simple idea:

> Understanding why is more valuable than memorizing how.

Every document, playbook, and future AI capability should reinforce that principle.

---

## Core Philosophy

OpsForge is based on the belief that great engineers solve problems through evidence, logical reasoning, and structured investigation.

Technology changes.

Engineering principles do not.

For that reason, OpsForge focuses on building engineering judgement rather than collecting commands.

---

## Design Principles

### 1. Understanding Before Action

Every recommendation should explain why it is being performed before describing how to perform it.

Users should understand the purpose of every troubleshooting step.

---

### 2. Evidence Before Repair

Repairs should never be based on assumptions.

Every action should be supported by evidence collected during the investigation.

---

### 3. Least Invasive First

Always begin with the safest and lowest-risk action capable of testing the current theory.

Escalate only when justified by evidence.

---

### 4. One Change at a Time

Avoid performing multiple repairs simultaneously.

Making one change at a time makes it possible to identify which action resolved the issue.

---

### 5. Verification Is Required

A repair is not complete until the original problem has been confirmed as resolved.

Running a command is not verification.

Successful operation is verification.

---

### 6. Learning Is Part of Troubleshooting

Every issue should increase the engineer's understanding of the underlying technology.

OpsForge is intended to teach engineering, not simply document solutions.

---

## Long-Term Vision

OpsForge is intended to become:

- A systems engineering knowledge base.
- A structured troubleshooting methodology.
- An AI-assisted engineering platform.
- A personal engineering portfolio.
- A lifelong learning resource.

The project should remain technology-agnostic, evidence-based, and understandable to engineers at every stage of their careers.
