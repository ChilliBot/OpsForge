# OpsForge Playbook Template

## Title

Use a clear name for the issue.

Example:

Windows Update Failure

---

## Purpose

Explain what this playbook helps diagnose or resolve.

---

## Supported Scope

List the systems, platforms, or conditions this playbook applies to.

Example:

- Windows 10
- Windows 11
- Enterprise-managed workstations

---

## Common Symptoms

List the symptoms that may lead a technician to use this playbook.

Examples:

- Update repeatedly fails
- Error code appears
- Update remains stuck
- System rolls back after restart

---

## Initial Questions

List the minimum questions needed before troubleshooting begins.

Examples:

- What is the exact error code?
- When did the issue begin?
- Were any changes made recently?
- What troubleshooting has already been attempted?

---

## Evidence to Collect

List the evidence needed to make a reliable decision.

Examples:

- Windows version
- Available disk space
- Event Viewer errors
- Update history
- DISM and SFC results

---

## Possible Causes

List common causes from most likely to least likely.

Each cause should be labeled as:

- Confirmed
- Likely
- Possible
- Unverified
- Ruled out

---

## Troubleshooting Steps

For each step, include:

### Step 1

**Action**

Describe the action.

**Why**

Explain why the step is being performed.

**Risk level**

Low, medium, or high.

**Administrator access**

State whether elevated permissions are required.

**Expected result**

Explain what the technician should see.

**Verification**

Explain how to confirm the result.

**Next decision**

Explain what to do depending on the result.

---

## Stop Conditions

State when troubleshooting should stop.

Examples:

- Hardware failure is suspected
- Data loss risk is present
- Production systems are affected
- Required permissions are unavailable
- Security concerns are discovered

---

## Escalation Criteria

Explain when the issue should be escalated and which team may need to own it.

---

## Resolution Verification

Explain how to confirm that the original problem is fully resolved.

---

## Documentation Output

Provide the information needed for:

- Incident notes
- Shift handoff
- Resolution summary
- Root Cause Analysis
- Escalation notes

---

## References

List approved documentation or internal references used to support the playbook.

Do not include sensitive company information in public versions.
